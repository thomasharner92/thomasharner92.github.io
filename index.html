About Me

My name is Thomas Harner, and I am currently living in Burke, VA. I graduated with a B.S. in Geographic Information Systems from James Madision University in 2015, and since then I have been working full-time as a Geographer for the US Army Corps of Engineers. I am also enrolled as a part-time student working towards my M.S. in GIS Web Map Programming with the University of Wisconsin-Madison.

The majority of my work consists of working with geospatial programming libraries to build attributed routing networks for Army vehicles and preferred Helicopter Landing Zone outputs.

As a current student, I have worked to build my web development capabilities, learning to build web maps using the MapBox, Leaflet, and ESRI APIs. Other coursework has included spatial database administration using PostrgreSQL and Python scripting.

In my spare time, I enjoy running, kayaking, and hiking around Northern Virginia and the Shenandoah Valley. My fiancee and I are also wine enthusiasts, having visited nearly 50 vineyards in the state of Virgina. Thank you for visiting, and feel free to view my resume below or browse my portfolio samples!
